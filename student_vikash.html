What was the origin of your enquiry? Where are you coming from on, what motivated you to take up this exploration? What are your philosophies around the idea? What motivated you towards this? What were some assumptions and notions you had? What was the idea or thought you came across?

When I stumbled upon braille, I realised that there has not been much attention been given to it unlike other mainstream commercial products/solutions. I wanted to look at braille as a probable design solution, and try to discover it's flaws and scope of improvement in it with the help of new technologies. A project called "ELIA Alphabet" motivated me as it was the starting point when I was certain about the scope in braille language.

I began my research with Podcast, articles, research paper and case studies shared by my peers and faculties. I noted the useful points on Miro that could inform my enquiry and make my assumptions visible for later reference. I also looked at existing designs so that I can find ways to improve on my idea.

I had to keep reminding myself that the studio was about speculation and not really about problem solving per say. So I had to take a pause from research, reflect on what is there and spend more time ideating a world without braille and designing life of blind person in that reality. I ideated a use-case scenario and tried to list down certain areas that I would be working on for during the make-a-thon.

In order to design a future without braille, I had to depend a lot on audio and tactile technology. The design ideas may not be very good by themselves, but they definitely have scope to solve some of issues of current design e.g. portability. The making exercise was surely was motivating insight for me. I also realised that it's not just a product that I needed to make, but also a system design in long run; which I'm pretty excited about to dig into.
