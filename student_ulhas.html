What was the origin of your enquiry? Where are you coming from on, what motivated you to take up this exploration? What are your philosophies around the idea? What motivated you towards this? What were some assumptions and notions you had? What was the idea or thought you came across?

I was initially looking at the following subjects - immersive experiences,gamification and consumerism.
I then chose advertising as it stands at the intersection of psychology,visual design and experience design - subjects that fascinate me to no end
I started from the viewpoint of the problem of consumerism in our society,but as I explored marketing practices more,I realized that ads are going to stay for years to come,but the way we interact with them and our relationships with them will evolve in vastly different ways



Did any author or fiction or philosophy inspire you? How did you go about exploring the inquiry you were interested in? How did you document the info you were coming across? How did you devise or plan an approach? Did you crush or prove any assumptions? How did you perform research?

I was fascinated by slavoj zizek's take on consumerism - how brands today make us actually feel good about being consumers, and how dangerous that strategy is.
Ambi Parameswaran's analysis of Indian ads over the decades (since independence) also influenced my thought process quite a bit.
I was also intrigued by Kunal Shah's (CRED) take on marketing and crafting memorable experiences which could offset dependencies on traditional advertising.
Digital ethnography
I looked at how startups are looking to disrupt,not just in terms of products, but also in the realm of marketing.

Put up your updated visual abstract and a brief summary of reflection

India has a rich history and our ads and films over the years have held a mirror to our society.Whether its a nobel prize or a political scam,ad campaigns have not shied away from speaking up on them.
As we move towards a hyper connected world, there is a growing worry that we're losing touch with our roots.Our diversity is what makes us Indian,and a lot of brands have managed to weave memorable stories around this,cutting across all sections of society.
In a country like India,where televsion and radio continue to be fairly prevalent in our homes,its important for brands to create effective storytelling along with envisioning curated experiences for urban consumers.
This also presents an exciting prospect of re-imagining advertising for rural markets - how can companies use hyper local traditions and practices and build on those platforms to woo potential consumers ? The UX here deviates from the standard notion of UI/UX design and brings with it new possibilities as the primary medium isn't only screens.
