Studio 1 journey has made me more conscious of my own personal biases and their potential impact on any design solution that I create. I wondered if it's possible to train people to avoid cognitive bias and whether it is even necessary. That is, 'Is "objective" design possible and necessarily the best outcome?" Also, I also came closer to understanding that "universal design" is a myth. For example, gamification of recruitment marginalises differently abled individuals and those that are on the low end of the digital divide spectrum. Should we strive for universal design? Or would this only lead to a less inclusive society?
